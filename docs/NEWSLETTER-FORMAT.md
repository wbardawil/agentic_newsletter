# Newsletter Format
*The seven-section structure of every issue. Fixed. Never changes.*

## Publication Name

**The Transformation Letter**
*(Placeholder — to be confirmed by publisher before launch)*

**Cadence:** Weekly. Same day. Same time. Non-negotiable.

**Platform:** Beehiiv

## The Seven Sections

The order is fixed: Signal first (the week's context), Apertura second
(Wadi's field observation), Insight third (the framework), Field Report
fourth (a named example), Tool fifth (the action), Compass sixth (the
forward signal), Door seventh (the invitation).

### Section 1 — THE SIGNAL
**Target length:** ~110 words (95–185 hard range)
**Purpose:** Anchor the issue in this week's corridor reality before any opinion is offered.

The Signal opens with an italicized thread sentence (15–20 words) that
names the single pattern connecting the week's four bullets. Then four
bullets, always in this order:

- **Strategy** — a move, deal, regulation, or market shift
- **Operating Models** — a structural decision at a named company (succession, reorg, process change)
- **Technology** — a system, tool, or integration shift that changes how operators work
- **Human Capital** — a hire, departure, or talent signal at the executive level

Each bullet: one sentence of fact, one sentence of implication for the
corridor operator, ending with `[Read ->](url)` linking to the source.
The bullets are evidence of the thread sentence. If a bullet does not
connect to the thread, it is replaced.

**What it is not:** A news roundup. A curated list of things you could
find elsewhere. A summary of everything interesting that happened.

**What it is:** The one pattern worth naming this week, proven in four
specific examples.

---

### Section 2 — THE APERTURA
**Target length:** ~100 words
**Purpose:** Establish that the writer is active in the field, not observing from a distance.

The Apertura is one real observation from Wadi's current work — a
pattern appearing across client conversations, a question being asked
by multiple business owners, something noticed this week in the
corridor market. It starts mid-thought, as if the reader is joining a
conversation already in progress.

**What it is not:** A summary of news. A curated take on someone else's
content. A hook engineered to generate opens. It is a genuine field
observation.

**Test:** Could this paragraph have been written without Wadi having
spoken to a business owner this week? If yes, replace it.

**Example structure:**
> "Three conversations this week. Different industries, different
> cities, different sizes. Same problem underneath. The owner is the
> most capable person in the building and that is why the building
> cannot grow..."

The pipeline produces 1–3 Apertura options (A=observation,
B=provocation, C=pattern) that Wadi picks from or edits before
publishing. The `apertura-history.json` file tracks which style Wadi
has chosen over time, so the Writer can match his register.

---

### Section 3 — THE INSIGHT
**Target length:** ~450 words
**Purpose:** Deliver one fully developed strategic idea from the Business Transformation OS.

This is the core of the issue. It lives inside exactly one of the three
OS pillars:
- **Strategy OS** — How the business thinks and decides
- **Operating Model OS** — How the business runs and scales
- **Technology OS** — How systems serve the strategy

**Structure (always in this order):**
1. **Problem** — Name the specific dysfunction, clearly, without softening
2. **Diagnosis** — The real cause beneath the visible symptom (what most advisors miss)
3. **Framework** — The transferable mental model from the Business Transformation OS
4. **Application** — One specific thing the reader can do this week

**Format rules:**
- Prose only. No bullet points. No numbered lists.
- The discipline of full sentences is non-negotiable in this section.
- At least one sentence should produce the aha moment: "I have never
  seen this named, but I have been living it."
- The framework must be applicable immediately, not contingent on
  hiring Wadi.

**The test for quality:** Would a reader pay to receive this insight?
If not, it is not finished.

---

### Section 4 — THE FIELD REPORT
**Target length:** ~150 words
**Purpose:** Demonstrate geographic and market fluency in the US-LATAM corridor.

One observation from the corridor — a company doing something worth
studying, a market pattern emerging, a regulatory or economic signal
that corridor operators should be watching. Brief. Factual. Pointed.
No more than 3–4 short paragraphs. The named example must be cited with
a source link.

**What it is not:** Aggregated news links. A press release summary.
Generic macroeconomic commentary.

**What it is:** The one thing Wadi noticed this week in the corridor
that a busy business owner might have missed, explained in its
operational implication — what it means for someone running a business
across the US-LATAM divide.

---

### Section 5 — THE TOOL
**Target length:** ~60 words
**Purpose:** Convert the Insight's framework into one concrete, named artifact the reader uses this week.

**Format:**
> **[Tool Name]** — what it is (one sentence). Why it matters to this
> operator (one sentence). Where to find it or how to build it (link
> or short description).

The Tool is tied to one of the four Signal pillars and is the action
step that follows the Insight. It can be a recommended external tool,
a one-page template Wadi names, or a specific diagnostic question
phrased as a repeatable exercise.

**Rules:**
- No affiliate links, no sponsored placements.
- Must apply to the $5M–$100M operator — not a startup tool, not an
  enterprise tool.
- If no strong external tool exists, derive one from the Insight (e.g.
  a named template, a one-page document, a diagnostic question).

---

### Section 6 — THE COMPASS
**Target length:** ~75 words
**Purpose:** Give the reader a forward-looking signal to track in the week ahead.

**Format:**
> **Watch for this week:** [one observable signal or tension to track].
> Then 2–3 sentences on why it matters and what it would signal if it
> moves.

The Compass is the gift at the end — a filter that lets the reader
notice something during the week that she would otherwise have missed.

**Rules:**
- Specific enough to act as a filter ("Watch for which vendors start
  requiring…").
- Not rhetorical. Not a restatement of the week's thesis.
- Not a rhetorical question. If you want to ask something, name the
  observable signal it would generate.

---

### Section 7 — THE DOOR
**Target length:** ~50 words
**Purpose:** Maintain a consistent, low-pressure invitation to engage or work together.

**Fixed text — never modified, never escalated:**

EN:
> *If something in this issue landed, reply — I read every response.*
>
> *If this is useful to someone in your network, forward it — it takes
> ten seconds and it's the highest compliment.*
>
> *When you're ready to work together directly, here is how we start:* [link]

ES:
> *Si algo de este número resonó contigo, respóndeme — leo cada mensaje.*
>
> *Si le es útil a alguien en tu red, reenvíalo — es el mayor cumplido.*
>
> *Cuando estés listo para trabajar juntos directamente, así es como
> empezamos:* [link]

**Rules:**
- This text does not change from issue to issue.
- No urgency, no scarcity, no discount.
- The link goes to the application/diagnostic page, not a sales page.
- The invitation is for a relationship, not a transaction.

## Issue QA Checklist

Before every issue is sent, confirm:

- [ ] The Signal opens with an italicized thread sentence followed by
      exactly 4 pillar bullets, each with a source link
- [ ] The Apertura traces to a real observation from this week, not
      synthesized content
- [ ] The Insight lives inside exactly one OS pillar (labeled clearly
      in the draft metadata)
- [ ] The Insight contains a Problem → Diagnosis → Framework →
      Application sequence, in prose, no bullets
- [ ] No banned phrases appear anywhere in the issue (see Voice Bible)
- [ ] The Field Report cites a named example with a source link
- [ ] The Tool is named, specific, and tied to one Signal pillar
- [ ] The Compass opens with "Watch for this week:" and names a
      trackable signal
- [ ] The Door text is unchanged
- [ ] Total word count is 1,000–1,200
- [ ] The Spanish edition is transcreated, not machine-translated
- [ ] The evaluation rubric (`docs/EVALUATION-RUBRIC.md`) was run and
      the edition scored ≥ 80 before send
- [ ] Issue was reviewed and approved by Wadi before send

## Bilingual Delivery Model

**English edition:** Sent Tuesday, 7:00am
**Spanish edition:** Sent Thursday, 7:00am (same content, fully transcreated)

Beehiiv language preference segmentation: subscribers select their
preferred language at signup. Each subscriber receives one edition
only — the one in their language. Neither edition contains the other
language inline.

The Spanish edition is not a translation. It is rewritten for a reader
whose business culture and idioms are rooted in Latin America.
Framework names (Strategy OS, Operating Model OS, Technology OS) remain
consistent across both editions. The full set of transcreation rules
lives in `config/prompts/localizer.md`.
